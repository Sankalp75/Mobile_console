Every once in a while, something comes along that makes you wonder — why didn't this exist before?

You have a phone in your pocket. It has a screen. It knows when you touch it, tilt it, shake it. It vibrates. It has buttons on the side. It's more powerful than the computers that sent people to the moon.

And yet, when you want to play a game on your PC, you go buy a piece of plastic.

**We think that's absurd.**

---

## The Problem

Game controllers haven't changed in 30 years. They're expensive. They're generic. They break. You forget them at a friend's place. And every single one of them assumes your hands are the same size, your games are the same genre, and your preferences don't matter.

You get what they give you.

## The Idea

What if the best game controller was the one you already own?

**Mobile Console** turns your phone into a fully customizable game controller for your PC. No app store. No Bluetooth pairing nightmares. No batteries to charge. Just plug in with a USB cable, and play.

That's it.

---

## How It Feels

You plug your phone into your PC. A controller appears on your screen. You press a button — and it just works. No lag. No setup wizard. No accounts.

It feels *instant*. Because it is.

We don't send your inputs over WiFi and hope for the best. We send them through the wire. The same USB cable you charge with. The result? Your phone responds faster than a wireless Xbox controller. Faster than a PlayStation DualSense. Faster than anything with Bluetooth.

**Under 6 milliseconds.** You press it. It happens.

---

## What Makes It Different

### It's yours.

Every button on your screen — you put it there. You decide how big it is, where it goes, what it does. Want six buttons? Two? Twenty? Your call.

Playing a racing game? Tilt your phone to steer. Playing a shooter? Use the gyroscope to aim — the same way the Steam Deck does it, but on a device you already own.

Every game gets its own layout. Every player gets their own feel.

### It knows your phone.

Big screen? Small screen? Notch? No notch? Tablet? It doesn't matter. Mobile Console looks at your device and adapts. Buttons scale. Layouts adjust. Features that your phone supports appear. Features it doesn't — disappear. No clutter. No confusion.

### It uses everything.

Your phone is full of sensors that nobody uses for gaming. We use all of them.

The **touchscreen** becomes your buttons, your joysticks, your triggers.
The **gyroscope** becomes your aim.
The **accelerometer** detects when you shake — reload, dodge, whatever you map it to.
The **volume buttons** become shoulder triggers you can actually *feel* click.
The **vibration motor** taps back on every press — crisp, precise, satisfying.

Your phone was always a game controller. It just didn't know it yet.

### It's completely offline.

No internet. No cloud. No telemetry. No ads. No accounts. No subscriptions.

It runs on your PC. Your phone connects over USB. Everything stays between you and your machine. Download it once, use it forever.

## Profiles

Your friend plays the same game differently than you do. That's fine.

Save your layout as a profile. Share the file. They drop it in and it works on their phone — automatically scaled, automatically adjusted.

Build a collection. One for racing. One for shooters. One for platformers. Switch between them in a tap.
